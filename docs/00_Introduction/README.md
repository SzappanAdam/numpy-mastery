# 📘 Chapter 0 – Introduction

Welcome to the beginning of your NumPy journey.

This chapter lays the foundation for everything that follows throughout the course. Before writing complex array operations or exploring high-performance numerical computing, it is essential to understand **what NumPy is, why it exists, and why it has become one of the most important libraries in the Python ecosystem**.

Many beginners rush directly into writing code. While that may produce working programs, it often leaves important conceptual gaps. This course takes a different approach.

Throughout this chapter, you will build an intuition for NumPy before diving into its syntax. By understanding the motivation behind the library, you'll find that many of its design decisions become natural and easier to remember.

---

# 🎯 Learning Objectives

After completing this chapter, you will be able to:

- Explain what NumPy is and why it was created.
- Understand the problems NumPy solves.
- Install NumPy correctly.
- Create your first NumPy array.
- Recognize the role of NumPy in the scientific Python ecosystem.
- Prepare your development environment for the rest of this course.

# 🧠 What Makes This Chapter Different?

This chapter intentionally focuses more on understanding than coding.

Instead of introducing dozens of functions immediately, we'll first answer questions such as:

- Why was NumPy created?
- Why isn't Python's built-in list enough?
- Why is NumPy significantly faster?
- What problems does NumPy solve?
- What is an ndarray?
- Why do nearly all scientific Python libraries depend on NumPy?

Once these ideas become clear, learning the rest of NumPy becomes much easier.
